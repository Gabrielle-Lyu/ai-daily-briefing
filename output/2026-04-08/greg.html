This follows a broader pattern of tech giants using project-finance structures to accelerate GPU cluster deployment without fully consuming balance sheet capacity. The implication: AI infrastructure capex is moving beyond equity-funded growth into structured credit markets, compressing the timeline for large-scale capacity additions across the industry.
As hyperscalers like Meta unlock cheap debt financing for AI data centers, the pace of competing GPU capacity coming online accelerates—raising the bar for OCI to differentiate on performance-per-dollar and sovereign/enterprise placement rather than raw capacity. This also validates the infrastructure investment thesis OCI is executing on, but the emergence of structured credit as a funding mechanism means well-capitalized competitors can scale faster than traditional capex cycles would suggest.
